# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**Product:** RootSpec — the developer-facing product surface (skills, validation, workflows)

**Scope:** This spec covers the product experience, not the framework definition. The framework grammar (`00.FRAMEWORK.md`) and foundational axioms (`00.AXIOMS.md`) are authored directly. This spec defines what using RootSpec should feel like.

**References:** [00.AXIOMS.md](00.AXIOMS.md) (external — axioms are pre-framework)

---

## Mission

Make specification the natural first step of building software — not a burden that competes with building, but the act that makes building reliable.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Guided Confidence

Developers always know what to do next and trust that the process will produce something sound.

**Developer perspective:** "I don't need to understand the framework to use it well. The skill is leading me somewhere, and I trust the direction."

### 2. Honest Feedback

Validation tells developers the truth about their spec — not what they want to hear, not vague encouragement. Scores are deterministic. Violations are named.

**Developer perspective:** "When it says my spec scores 72, I know exactly what's wrong and where to fix it. There's no ambiguity."

### 3. Progressive Depth

Simple things are simple. Complex things are possible. A developer creating their first spec and a team maintaining a mature product both find the right level of engagement.

**Developer perspective:** "I started with `/rs-init` and `/rs-spec` and had a working L1-L5 in one session. Six months later I'm using `/rs-validate` to catch regressions. I didn't have to learn everything upfront."

### 4. Invisible Structure

The framework's rules (hierarchy, references, single source of truth) are enforced through the experience, not explained as prerequisites. Developers follow the rules because the skills guide them through the rules — not because they read a spec about specs.

**Developer perspective:** "I've never read 00.FRAMEWORK.md. I just use the skills and my spec is valid."

## Inviolable Principles

- Never require developers to understand the framework to use it correctly
- Never produce validation results that require interpretation — scores and violations must be self-explanatory
- Never add workflow steps that don't directly serve spec quality or developer understanding
- The interview is a conversation, not a form — challenge bad input, don't just accept it

## Anti-Pillars

What RootSpec's developer experience is NOT:

- **Not a generator.** RootSpec extracts and validates — it does not invent product vision on behalf of the developer.
- **Not a gatekeeper.** Validation identifies problems; it does not block work. Developers choose when and how to fix.
- **Not comprehensive upfront.** A partial spec that's honest about gaps is better than a complete spec that's wrong.
